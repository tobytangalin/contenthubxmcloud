Privacy checklist

Abstract

Guide for assessing how your organization is supporting individuals' data privacy in your XM Cloud implementation.

Warning

This Privacy Guide provides technical guidance on how your developers can choose to configure your Sitecore product implementation to support you with data privacy compliance. This guide does not provide exhaustive guidance, and should not be construed or used as legal advice about the content, interpretation, or application of any law or regulation. You, the customer, will always be in the best position to assess your own risks, and must seek your own legal counsel to understand the applicability of any law or regulation to your business, including how you process personal information. Your resulting implementation is based entirely on your own configuration choices.

Use this privacy checklist as a starting point for assessing how your organization is supporting individuals' data privacy.

Initial review

Consider where and how you are storing data in the platform:

  • Consider whether you need to request consent to store and process personal information and plan to persist consent choices.

  • Consider how you will renew consent if your current process needs to change.

  • Limit the exposure of personal information throughout the platform. You can, for example, choose not to write personal information to logs.

  • Internally document the types of personal information you are storing.

  • Review synchronization of data between your Sitecore implementation and third-party applications and ensure it is included in the privacy policy. Only synchronize data when necessary.

Individual data rights

Consider whether your implementation supports individuals' data privacy. For example:

  • Individuals can request that their personal information is deleted, either by contacting the organization or using a self-service portal.

  • Individuals can request a copy of their data, either by contacting the organization or using a self-service portal.

  • Individuals can update their personal information, either by contacting the organization or using a self-service portal. Make sure that the user is able to view and update each record individually.

  • Individuals are given clear information about how the organization processes and stores personal information. From a development point of view, ensure that it is easy for business users to update privacy policies and privacy warnings.

  • Review the consents or permissions in place prior to any synchronization to or from third-party applications.

  • Privacy policies describe the ways in which data is stored and processed across the entire platform. Consider how your organization stores personal data.

  • Consider how individuals are informed of any new forms of processing as you enable them or add them to the platform.

  • Determine how consent choices are persisted.

  • Provide the ability for individuals to be able to revoke consent at any time, for example, by contacting the organization or using an online form.

  • Processing can be disabled for individuals who have not given consent, have revoked consent, or have objected to processing. Alternatively, if you are unable to disable processing, ensure that you no longer store an individual's information.

  • Prohibit data practices that might be considered discriminatory.

Access

  • Ensure that you have limited access to user interfaces to users that require access.

Selling of personal information

  • Ensure that you have included the ability for the individual to opt-out from selling their personal information.

Storing request history

  • Consider how you enable an individual to their request history and how you store these requests to ensure the individual does not face discrimination.

  • Review the request history to ensure that decisions around the segmentation of data does not negatively impact the user interface or customer experience.
